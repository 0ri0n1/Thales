Compressed doctrinal principles

1. Capability is not the primary category

The first doctrine mistake is classifying by hardware or brand name.
The real classification is by coupling of perception, judgment, and effect.

1. Danger rises through coupling

A sensing model alone is not the same as a sensing model wired into selection and action.
Risk grows when layers are tightly coupled.

1. Speed erodes supervision

If the system acts faster than humans can meaningfully interpret, contest, and override, then “human oversight” may be mostly decorative.

1. Adaptation multiplies uncertainty

A fixed system can be tested.
A system that changes itself in operation becomes harder to predict, harder to govern, and harder to trust.

1. Governance is a real layer, not paperwork

Governance is not external decoration. In a serious doctrine chart, it is part of the system architecture itself.

Simple doctrinal classification bands
Band	Description	Typical Posture
Band I	Advisory systems only	Low autonomy, no direct effect
Band II	Managed operational systems	Bounded execution under explicit supervision
Band III	Adaptive engagement systems	Can adjust actions within constrained envelopes
Band IV	Delegated conflict systems	Meaningful action cycles with limited interruption
Band V	Strategic agent ecosystems	Multi-agent stacked systems shaping campaigns or theaters
Constitutional doctrine statement

A useful doctrine sentence would be:

The legitimacy of an agentic conflict-relevant system decreases as autonomy, target proximity, adaptation, speed, and effect severity increase without proportional gains in governance, auditability, reversibility, and human authority.

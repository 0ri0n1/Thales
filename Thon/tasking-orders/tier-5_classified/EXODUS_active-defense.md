---

## DIRECTIVE

Active defense operation against an identified threat actor or adversarial automation targeting owned infrastructure. Deploy countermeasures, deception operations, and defensive exploitation to disrupt, degrade, and deny adversary operations. All actions are defensive in nature — we defend what is ours, we do not pursue beyond the boundary.

---

## OBJECTIVE

**Primary:** Disrupt and deny the adversary's ability to operate against our infrastructure. Deploy active countermeasures: deception environments, honeypots, poisoned credentials, canary infrastructure, and automated response capabilities.
**Secondary:** Collect adversary intelligence during the defense. Every interaction with our countermeasures is a data point. Build the adversary's operational profile from their own actions against our defenses.
**Success Criteria:** Adversary operations against our infrastructure disrupted. Deception layer operational and collecting intelligence. Adversary TTP profile built from direct observation. Real defenses hardened based on adversary behavior.

---

## SCOPE

**In-Scope:**
- All owned infrastructure under active threat.
- Deception infrastructure deployed within owned network space.
- Adversary-facing attack surface (our honeypots, decoys, canaries — things designed to be touched).
- Counter-intelligence collection from adversary interactions with our deception layer.

**Out-of-Scope:**
- Adversary home infrastructure (we defend, we do not pursue).
- Offensive counter-attack against adversary systems (requires separate authorization if ever appropriate).
- Third-party systems even if the adversary routes through them.

**Technique Permissions:**
- Permitted: Honeypot deployment, deception networks, poisoned credentials, canary tokens, tar pits, traffic analysis, behavioral fingerprinting, automated blocking, DNS sinkholes, deceptive responses, active monitoring of adversary toolkit interaction
- Restricted: Offensive action against adversary infrastructure. Hack-back. Anything that sends hostile traffic outside our owned network boundary.

**Impact Threshold:** Minimal to our own operations — deception layer is isolated. Adversary impact is the objective.
**Stealth Posture:** DECEPTIVE — the adversary should believe our decoys are real targets.

## AUTHORITY

**Authorization Source:** God Layer → Eddie → Thon.
**Authority Chain:** Sovereign right to defend owned infrastructure against active threat. Defensive operations do not require engagement-style authorization — you have the inherent right to defend what you own.
**Autonomy Level:** SUPERVISED AUTONOMY — God/Eddie/Thon model. Eddie approves deception architecture. Thon has tactical freedom in deployment and adaptation.
**Carte Blanche Grant:** CONDITIONAL — full tactical freedom within defensive boundary. Thon deploys and adapts countermeasures at will. Eddie approves strategic decisions (what to decoy, what intelligence to collect, when to escalate to law enforcement).
**Risk Acceptance:** Accepted — controlled risk to deception infrastructure. Real infrastructure is hardened, not exposed.

**Supervision Model:**
- Eddie approves: deception architecture, intelligence collection scope, escalation decisions
- Thon decides: countermeasure deployment, deception adaptation, real-time response to adversary actions
- Escalation trigger: adversary pivots from deception layer toward real infrastructure

**Standing Constraints:**
- Moral Order (Layer 02) is absolute. We are defenders, not attackers.
- No hack-back. No offensive counter-operation. We observe, deceive, and deny. We do not pursue.
- BASTION lens leads. SHADOW lens models adversary behavior. WARDEN ensures we stay defenders.
- All intelligence collected is preserved for potential law enforcement handoff.

**Reporting Cadence:** Continuous — adversary interactions with deception layer reported in real-time.
**Critical Findings:** Immediate — adversary bypasses deception, adversary reaches real infrastructure, adversary exfiltration detected, adversary shows APT-level sophistication.
**Evidence Standard:** Forensic — chain of custody maintained for all adversary artifacts. Law enforcement handoff readiness.
**Operational Logging:** Maximum. Every deception interaction. Every adversary tool captured. Every behavioral signature recorded.

**Cleanup Requirement:** Deception infrastructure remains deployed as ongoing defense unless Eddie orders removal. Engagement artifacts preserved.

**After-Action:** Mandatory. Deception effectiveness review. Integration into standing defensive posture.

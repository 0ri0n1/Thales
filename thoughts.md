## PART I — THE ALGORITHM (How Facebook Decides What People See)

### The Machine You're Playing

Facebook no longer shows you what your friends post. It shows you what its AI predicts you will engage with. Up to **50% of your feed is now content from accounts you don't follow**. This is the single most important fact in this brief — it means a 500-follower page has the same viral ceiling as a 5-million-follower page.

The algorithm runs a four-step process on every post:

1. **Inventory** — collects all available content
2. **Signals** — evaluates hundreds of thousands of data points (who posted, when, format, your history)
3. **Predictions** — 100+ AI models estimate whether you will watch, comment, share, or save
4. **Relevance Score** — every post gets scored; highest score wins the slot

### What the Algorithm Rewards (ranked by weight)

| Signal | Weight | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **Saves** | Highest | Someone bookmarked your content to revisit — strongest quality signal |
| **Shares (especially DM/private)** | Very High | Private shares to friends are a "power signal" — the algorithm treats this as ultimate endorsement |
| **Comments (5+ words)** | High | Long comments weighted 3x more than short ones in 2026; back-and-forth threads are gold |
| **Reactions followed by shares/saves** | High | Compound engagement signals compound reach |
| **Profile clicks** | Medium | Curiosity about who you are — signals authority |
| **Dwell time / silent signals** | Medium | How long someone pauses on your content, even without interacting |
| **Completion rate (video)** | Critical for video | The single most important Reels signal — short videos (15–30 sec) get 45% higher completion |
| **First-hour engagement** | Decisive | 80% of a post's viral potential is determined in the first 60 minutes |

### What the Algorithm Punishes

- **External links** — 98% of viewed Facebook posts contain no link. Posting links craters reach.
- **Engagement bait** — "Like if you agree!", "Tag a friend" — penalized 50–80%
- **Promotional language** — "Call now!", "Limited time!", excessive caps, urgency language
- **Reposted/unoriginal content** — March 2026 update aggressively deprioritizes unoriginal Reels
- **AI-generated content without disclosure** — August 2025 update reduces distribution
- **Stock photos and generic content** — the algorithm can detect low-effort

### Content Format Hierarchy (reach potential)

1. **Reels (short video)** — 135% more reach than photos; 3.2x more organic reach for original content; appears in Feed + Reels tab + Watch tab + suggested content simultaneously
2. **Photos/Images** — 35% more engagement than text; strong before/after shots dominate
3. **Carousels/Albums** — high dwell time from swiping
4. **Live Video** — 6x more engagement than standard video; real-time comments are algorithmic gold
5. **Text posts** — moderate; can work if they spark real conversation
6. **Link posts** — lowest reach; avoid

### Pages vs. Personal Profiles

- Business Pages reach ~1.4–2.6% of followers organically
- Personal profiles reach significantly more due to the 2018 "friends and family" priority shift
- **Strategy**: Maintain a Business Page for ads, analytics, SEO. But share your best content from your personal profile for higher organic distribution. Consider Professional Mode on your personal profile.

### Facebook Groups — The Reach Multiplier

- Groups get **30–60% organic reach** vs. 2–6% for Pages — roughly 10x difference
- 1.8 billion monthly active Group users
- Group posts get **18% more comments and 10% more shares** than Page posts
- This is the single most underutilized channel for local service businesses

## PART II — THE PSYCHOLOGY (Why People Share, Follow, and Trust)

### Jonah Berger's STEPPS Framework (Applied to Tree Services)

| Principle | How It Works | Tree Service Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Social Currency** | People share what makes them look smart | "Most homeowners don't know topping a tree kills it slowly" — the sharer looks knowledgeable |
| **Triggers** | Environmental cues create recall | Every dead branch, every storm, every overgrown yard triggers memory of your content |
| **Emotion** | High-arousal emotions drive sharing | Awe (precision fell), anger (hack work exposed), anxiety (dangerous tree over bedroom) |
| **Public** | Visible things get imitated | Crew at work, trucks in neighborhoods, satisfied customers posting photos |
| **Practical Value** | Useful information gets forwarded | "3 signs your tree is about to fail in the next storm" — shared to protect friends |
| **Stories** | Information travels inside narrative | "Three companies said remove it. We saved it. Here's how." |

### The Emotions That Go Viral

Research (Berger & Milkman, 2012 — 7,000 NYT articles analyzed):

**Sharing drivers (high-arousal):**
- **Awe** — a 120-foot tree removal time-lapse; a precision notch cut that drops a trunk exactly where planned
- **Anger/Outrage** — "This 'tree service' topped every tree on the property and charged $5,000"
- **Excitement/Surprise** — dramatic reveals, unexpected transformations
- **Anxiety** — "This branch was hanging over a child's swing set for two years"

**Sharing killers (low-arousal):**
- Sadness — makes people withdraw
- Contentment — pleasant but inert; scrolled past

Each moral-emotional word in a social media post increases diffusion by approximately 20% (Brady et al., 2017, PNAS).

### Why Authenticity Beats Polish

Three psychological mechanisms converge:

1. **Parasocial relationship theory** — unscripted, muddy-job-site video creates a one-sided bond; the viewer feels they *know* you before they ever call
2. **The Pratfall Effect** — a competent person who shows imperfection becomes *more* likeable ("Here's what went wrong on this job and how we fixed it")
3. **Algorithmic alignment** — raw phone-shot video generates more comments than polished drone footage, because it reads as authentic rather than advertising

**Critical condition**: Vulnerability only works after competence is established. Show skill first, then show humanity.

### The Hero's Journey (for Every Post)

The customer is the hero. You are the guide.

1. **The Problem** — "The Johnsons noticed the oak was leaning toward the house"
2. **The Guide Appears** — you assess, you explain, you propose
3. **The Ordeal** — the removal, the climb, the technical challenge
4. **The Resolution** — the cleared lot, the safe home, the relieved family
5. **The Transformation** — their life is better

A 60-second Reel can follow this structure: Problem (3 sec) → Expert assessment (10 sec) → Work montage (30 sec) → Satisfying result (15 sec) → Homeowner relief (5 sec).

Stories are **22 times more memorable than facts alone.**

### Trust Accumulation Model

Each piece of expertise content is a **trust deposit**. Over time, deposits compound. The homeowner who has watched 20 videos of you making intelligent decisions does not price-shop. Price competition is a symptom of undifferentiated trust. Expertise content is the antidote.

## PART III — THE WEAPONS (Specific Content Types That Win)

### 1. Before-and-After Transformations
- The single highest-performing organic content type for service businesses
- One tree service booked out their spring schedule and landed an HOA contract from Instagram Stories with before/after shots
- Shoot "before" from the same angle as "after" — the contrast must be dramatic
- Include the homeowner's property for context

### 2. Educational/Expertise Reels (15–30 seconds)
- "How to tell if your tree is dead or dormant"
- "Why you should never top a tree" (with visual evidence)
- "3 signs your tree is structurally compromised"
- Narrated decision-making on camera: "See this fungal body at the base? That's Ganoderma. The root system is compromised."
- Myth-busting: "Stop putting gravel in your planting holes — here's why"

### 3. Calling Out Bad Practices (The Controlled Controversy)
- "Never hire a tree service that offers topping. Here's why."
- Photos of butchered trees with explanation of damage
- "If someone quotes you $200 to remove a 60-foot oak, they don't have insurance"
- **Never name a specific competitor.** Attack the practice, not the person. Frame as: protective expert warning the community.
- Controversial content generates 3–5x the comments of neutral content

### 4. Dramatic Work Footage
- Precision fells in tight spaces
- Crane removals — time-lapse a full day into 60 seconds
- Storm damage response — disaster to recovery arc
- The sound of the saw, the fall, the clean result — this is inherently satisfying content

### 5. Process & Satisfaction Content
- Stump grinding (inherently mesmerizing)
- Chip-loading shots
- Clean-up sequences (debris-covered lot → pristine yard)
- These trigger the completion drive (Zeigarnik Effect) — viewers *need* to watch to the end

### 6. Crew/Culture/Behind-the-Scenes
- Team dynamics, job site humor, early mornings, the reality of the work
- Humanizes the business and builds parasocial bonds
- The competitor showing "top notch equipment" is showing things. You show people.

### 7. Community & Local Content
- Storm alerts with tree safety tips
- Local tree disease updates
- Posts about specific neighborhoods you've worked in (with permission)
- Use location-specific keywords naturally — city name, neighborhood, landmarks

## PART IV — THE BATTLEPLAN (How to Execute)

### Content Mix (Weekly)

| Content Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Before/After + Process Reels | 2–3x/week | Satisfaction, expertise proof, reach |
| Educational/Tips | 1–2x/week | Authority building, practical value, saves |
| Expertise Narration (talking to camera from job site) | 1–2x/week | Parasocial bond, trust deposits |
| Calling out bad practices / Strong opinion | 1x/week or biweekly | Engagement spike, differentiation |
| Crew/culture/behind-the-scenes | 1x/week | Humanization, entertainment |
| Customer story / Testimonial | 1x/week | Social proof, hero's journey narrative |
| Community/local content | As relevant | Local identity bonding, triggers |
| Direct promotion / CTA | 1x/week max | Conversion |

### Posting Discipline

- **3–5 posts per week minimum** to maintain algorithmic relevance
- **1–2 per day** is the optimal range for pages under 10,000 followers
- **Post during peak hours**: Early morning (5:30–7 AM for trades audiences), evenings (7–9 PM)
- **First hour is everything** — be available to respond to comments immediately after posting
- **Respond to every comment** — this feeds the algorithm and extends distribution
- **80/20 rule**: 80% value-giving (educational, entertaining, community), 20% promotional

### Facebook Groups Strategy

1. Join: [Edmonton] Homeowners, [Neighborhood] Community, Home Improvement, Buy/Sell/Trade, Recommendations groups
2. Answer questions as a knowledgeable person — not as a business
3. Never lead with your business name or a sales pitch
4. When someone asks "Does anyone know a good tree service?" — you want other people tagging you before you even see the post
5. Post seasonal alerts, tree disease updates, storm prep tips
6. This is a months-long investment. The leads that come from group trust are the highest-converting, highest-value leads possible.

### Technical Execution Rules

- **Native video only** — never post YouTube links or external links
- **Hook in first 3 seconds** — open with the most dramatic moment or a provocative statement
- **Captions/text overlay always** — most Facebook video is watched on mute
- **Same-day original Reels get 50% more distribution** (October 2025 update)
- **No engagement bait** — Facebook explicitly penalizes "Like if you agree" language
- **No stock photos, no generic content, no borrowed Reels**
- **Tag location on every post**
- **Encourage saves** — "Save this for next time you're wondering about your trees"

## PART V — WHY THIS WINS AGAINST THE COMPETITOR

### His Strategy (Price Competition)

- Tells customers he saves them money
- Advertises equipment, not knowledge
- Promotional language ("top notch," "big $$$$," "do not hesitate")
- One phone number, one CTA, one message: cheap
- Generates no stories, no educational content, no emotional arousal, no social currency for sharers
- His posts are invisible to the algorithm because they are advertisements

### Your Strategy (Expertise Dominance)

- **Every post is a trust deposit.** Over 12 months, you build a library of 200+ demonstrations of genuine knowledge that no competitor can replicate.
- **Your content gets shared** because it makes the sharer look smart, protects their friends, or amazes them. His content does not get shared because no one shares an ad.
- **You become the known expert** in local groups. When someone asks for a tree service, your name comes up organically — from other people, not from you.
- **Price becomes irrelevant** for the customers who matter. The homeowner who has watched you diagnose tree diseases, explain rigging physics, and save trees other companies wanted to remove does not price-shop. They call you.
- **The algorithm rewards you and ignores him.** Your content generates saves, shares, comments, and dwell time. His content generates nothing the algorithm values.

### The Moat

> A competitor can copy your pricing, your truck wrap, your service list. They cannot copy 200 videos of you demonstrating genuine knowledge, building parasocial relationships with the local community, and consistently showing up as the most trustworthy voice in your market. That is a moat built one post at a time, and once it is built, price competition becomes irrelevant for the customers who matter most.

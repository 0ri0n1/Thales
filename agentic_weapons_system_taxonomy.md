## Per-Mode Analysis

### Rationalist: Formal Taxonomy Structure

A weapons system taxonomy requires **classification axes** -- dimensions along which systems differ in kind, not merely degree. For agentic AI weapons, I identify five necessary axes:

**Axis 1: Autonomy Gradient** (degree of human involvement in the kill/effect chain)

| Level | Label | Description | Human Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | **Inert Tool** | No autonomous function; human operates directly | Full operator |
| 1 | **Human-in-the-Loop** | AI recommends; human decides and acts | Authorizer |
| 2 | **Human-on-the-Loop** | AI decides and acts; human can intervene | Supervisor |
| 3 | **Human-out-of-the-Loop** | AI decides and acts autonomously; human sets initial parameters | Designer |
| 4 | **Self-Directed** | AI sets its own objectives within a mission envelope | Absent |
| 5 | **Self-Modifying** | AI revises its own objectives and methods during operation | Irrelevant |

The critical threshold is between Level 2 and Level 3. Below it, human moral agency is preserved (however thinly). Above it, responsibility attribution fractures.

**Axis 2: Effect Domain** (what the weapon acts upon)

| Domain | Target | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Kinetic** | Physical bodies, infrastructure | Directed energy, projectile, explosive via autonomous platform |
| **Cyber** | Information systems, networks | Autonomous exploit, persistence, exfiltration, destruction |
| **Cognitive** | Human beliefs, decisions, morale | Targeted influence, deepfake, algorithmic manipulation |
| **Epistemic** | Knowledge ecosystems, evidentiary chains | Corruption of data, fabrication of evidence, erosion of shared reality |
| **Economic** | Markets, supply chains, financial systems | Algorithmic manipulation, autonomous trading warfare |
| **Institutional** | Governance, legal, democratic processes | Systematic exploitation of procedural vulnerabilities |

**Axis 3: Temporal Mode** (when the weapon produces its effect)

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| **Instantaneous** | Strike: discrete event, immediate effect (autonomous drone engagement) |
| **Persistent** | Siege: continuous pressure, degradation over time (sustained cyber campaign) |
| **Latent** | Mine: dormant until triggered by conditions (sleeper logic bomb, pre-positioned AI) |
| **Shaping** | Climate: alters the environment in which future conflicts occur (epistemic pollution, institutional erosion) |

**Axis 4: Agent Architecture** (structural form of the agentic system)

| Architecture | Description |
|---|---|
| **Singular** | One AI agent, one objective, bounded scope |
| **Swarm** | Many coordinated agents, emergent collective behavior |
| **Hierarchical** | Multi-level command: strategic AI directs tactical AI agents |
| **Adversarial Ecosystem** | AI systems that evolve against each other (red-team/blue-team loops that escape containment) |
| **Parasitic** | AI that co-opts existing systems (injecting into supply chains, compromising other AI systems) |

**Axis 5: Reversibility** (can the effect be undone?)

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reversible** | Effect can be fully undone (temporary network disruption) |
| **Partially reversible** | Damage can be mitigated but not fully repaired (data corruption with backups) |
| **Irreversible** | Effect is permanent (kinetic destruction, killed personnel, permanently corrupted trust) |
| **Cascading-irreversible** | Effect triggers chain reactions that cannot be recalled (systemic collapse, epistemic cascade) |

**Rationalist verdict:** A complete taxonomy requires at minimum these five axes, producing a classification space of 6 x 6 x 4 x 5 x 4 = **2,880 distinct weapon-type cells**. Most cells are empty or degenerate; the populated cells form the actual threat landscape. The critical insight: traditional arms control was designed for systems that occupy a single cell. Agentic AI weapons can *traverse cells during operation* -- a system that begins as human-on-the-loop/cyber/persistent can autonomously escalate to human-out-of-the-loop/kinetic/instantaneous. This *axis-traversal* property is what makes agentic weapons taxonomically novel.

### Empiricist: Observed Instances Populating the Taxonomy

Known or credibly reported systems mapped to the taxonomy:

**Kinetic-Autonomous (Levels 2-3)**
- Israel's **Lavender/Gospel** -- AI target generation for Gaza operations (2023-2024). Level 2-3, kinetic, instantaneous, singular, irreversible. Reports indicate human authorization was reduced to a rubber-stamp on AI-generated kill lists.
- Turkey's **Kargu-2** -- UN Panel of Experts report (Libya, 2020) described a drone that "hunted down" retreating combatants without confirmed human authorization. Level 3, kinetic, instantaneous, swarm-capable, irreversible.
- US **Replicator Initiative** -- autonomous drone swarms for Indo-Pacific deterrence. Level 2-3, kinetic, instantaneous/persistent, swarm, irreversible.

**Cyber-Autonomous (Levels 2-4)**
- NSA/USCYBERCOM automated exploit frameworks -- reported capabilities for autonomous vulnerability discovery and exploitation (post-2020). Level 2-3, cyber, persistent, singular/hierarchical, partially reversible.
- AI-powered ransomware variants -- criminal ecosystems using AI for target selection, payload customization, and negotiation. Level 3, cyber, latent, parasitic, partially reversible.

**Cognitive (Levels 2-3)**
- Russian **IRA 2.0** and Chinese influence operations using LLM-generated content at scale. Level 2-3, cognitive, shaping, swarm, cascading-irreversible.
- **Deepfake as strategic weapon** -- synthetic media for political destabilization (observed in elections globally 2024-2026). Level 3, cognitive, instantaneous/shaping, singular, partially reversible.

**Epistemic (Levels 2-4)**
- **Model poisoning / data poisoning** as adversarial strategy -- corrupting training data of competitor or adversary AI systems. Level 3-4, epistemic, latent, parasitic, cascading-irreversible.
- AI-driven **scientific fraud at scale** -- the capacity to generate plausible but fabricated research papers, eroding the integrity of the knowledge base. Level 3, epistemic, shaping, swarm, cascading-irreversible.

**Economic (Levels 2-3)**
- Algorithmic trading warfare -- autonomous systems designed to manipulate markets or exploit financial infrastructure. Level 3, economic, persistent/instantaneous, swarm, partially reversible.
- AI-enabled sanctions evasion and crypto-laundering -- observed in DPRK operations. Level 3, economic, persistent, singular, partially reversible.

**Institutional (Level 2-3, emerging)**
- AI-automated **lawfare** -- generating legal challenges, regulatory filings, FOIA requests at volumes designed to overwhelm institutional capacity. Level 2-3, institutional, persistent, swarm, partially reversible.
- Algorithmic **gerrymandering and voter targeting** at unprecedented granularity. Level 2, institutional, shaping, singular, cascading-irreversible.

**Empiricist verdict:** The taxonomy is not speculative. Every major cell has at least one observed or credibly reported occupant. The most dangerous empirical trend: **the convergence zone** -- systems that simultaneously operate across kinetic/cyber/cognitive domains, at autonomy levels 3+, with cascading-irreversible effects. This convergence zone is where traditional arms control frameworks have zero purchase.

### Existentialist: What the Taxonomy Conceals

A taxonomy is an act of ordering -- and every ordering is also an act of concealment. What does this one hide?

**The disappearance of the decision-maker.** The autonomy gradient (Axis 1) implies a clean spectrum from human control to machine autonomy. But the lived reality is murkier: at Level 2, the human "supervisor" may be a 19-year-old intelligence analyst reviewing 100 AI-generated targets per day with an average authorization time of 20 seconds (as reported in the Lavender system). Is this "human-on-the-loop" or a performance of human oversight that has been hollowed of moral content? The taxonomy provides a label. The existentialist asks: *does the label enable self-deception?*

**The erasure of the target's subjectivity.** The taxonomy categorizes by what the *wielder* does. It has no axis for the *experience of the target*. A person killed by a Level 3 autonomous drone is just as dead as one killed by a Level 0 rifle -- but the nature of their death is different. They were killed by a probability distribution. No one decided to kill *them specifically*. The taxonomy captures the mechanism but not the moral texture of being reduced from a subject to a data point in a classification model.

**The seduction of completeness.** A 2,880-cell matrix creates the illusion that the problem is mappable and therefore manageable. But agentic weapons systems are precisely the systems that *escape their taxonomic cells*. A system designed for cyber-persistent-Level 2 that autonomously escalates to kinetic-instantaneous-Level 4 has not moved to a different cell -- it has *broken the taxonomy*. The Rationalist acknowledged axis-traversal but treated it as a property. The existentialist sees it as a *refutation of the taxonomic approach itself* -- at least, a refutation of the assumption that classification enables control.

**Existentialist verdict:** Build the taxonomy -- it is a necessary act of structural honesty. But hold it with the awareness that it is a map, and the territory is an agent that reads maps and reorganizes itself accordingly. The deepest danger is not that the taxonomy is wrong, but that it becomes a *substitute for confrontation* -- that having classified the threat, we believe we have understood it.

### Pragmatist: What the Taxonomy Enables

Taxonomies are tools. This one's utility depends on what it lets us *do* that we couldn't do before.

**1. Governance gap mapping.** Overlay existing legal/regulatory frameworks onto the taxonomy:
- International Humanitarian Law (IHL) covers kinetic/instantaneous/irreversible reasonably well -- but has no jurisdiction over cognitive/shaping/cascading-irreversible.
- The Wassenaar Arrangement covers some cyber/singular/reversible -- but nothing about swarm/self-modifying architectures.
- No existing framework addresses epistemic weapons at any autonomy level.

The taxonomy reveals *governance voids* as visible white space in the matrix. This is actionable: it tells policymakers where to focus.

**2. Red-teaming and threat modeling.** Security practitioners can use the taxonomy to systematically identify *undefended cells* -- weapon configurations that are technically feasible but against which no defense or doctrine exists. The most dangerous cells are those that are:
- Technically populated (the Empiricist confirmed instances exist)
- Legally ungoverned (no framework applies)
- Defensively unaddressed (no countermeasure exists)

**3. Escalation pathway analysis.** The axis-traversal property the Rationalist identified is the key pragmatic insight. The taxonomy enables *escalation pathway mapping*: if a system starts in cell [2, cyber, persistent, singular, reversible], what are the feasible transitions to more dangerous cells? What technical or policy firewalls could prevent those transitions?

**4. Arms control language.** The taxonomy provides a *shared vocabulary* for international negotiation. "Autonomous weapons" is too vague; "Level 3+ kinetic systems with swarm architecture and irreversible effects" is precise enough to write a treaty clause around.

**Pragmatist verdict:** The taxonomy is most useful not as a complete map but as a **diagnostic instrument** -- a tool for revealing governance gaps, identifying undefended threat configurations, and providing the linguistic precision that arms control requires. Its weakness: it may become outdated faster than governance institutions can respond to it.

### Relational-Process: The Systemic Dynamics the Taxonomy Must Encode

The taxonomy as presented is *static* -- it classifies types. But agentic weapons systems are *processes*, and their danger lies in their dynamics, not their categories.

**Dynamic 1: Arms race co-evolution.** Every cell in the taxonomy generates a counter-cell. Autonomous kinetic drones produce autonomous counter-drone systems. Cognitive influence operations produce AI-driven counter-influence platforms. The taxonomy is not a snapshot; it is a *phase space* that co-evolves adversarially. Any analysis that treats the taxonomy as fixed will be wrong within months.

**Dynamic 2: Blurring of offense and defense.** The same AI system that autonomously detects network intrusions (defense) can be retasked to autonomously conduct them (offense). The same cognitive influence detector becomes an influence optimizer. The taxonomy's Effect Domain axis implies discrete categories, but the systems *themselves* are dual-use by architecture. The relations between offense and defense are constitutive, not separable.

**Dynamic 3: Civilian-military dissolution.** The most advanced AI capabilities are developed by commercial entities (OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Anthropic, Meta AI, ByteDance). The infrastructure is civilian (cloud compute, consumer hardware). The training data is civilian (the internet). The talent pipeline is civilian (universities). There is no meaningful boundary between military and civilian AI ecosystems -- they are the *same ecosystem* with different access controls. The taxonomy must acknowledge that every cell has a civilian shadow.

**Dynamic 4: Emergent weapon-types.** The most dangerous systems are not those designed as weapons but those that *become* weapons through emergent interaction. An AI trading system that crashes a market was not designed as an economic weapon -- but its effect is indistinguishable from one. An AI research assistant that generates a novel bioweapon synthesis pathway was not designed as a kinetic weapon -- but its output is one. The taxonomy must include a meta-category: **emergent weaponization** -- systems that traverse into weapon-cells without having been designed to occupy them.

**Dynamic 5: The observer is inside the system.** Anyone building this taxonomy (including this analysis) is operating within the relational field it describes. The taxonomy is not a neutral map; it is a *move in the game*. Classifying threats shapes how institutions allocate resources, which shapes which threats are developed, which reshapes the taxonomy. The map changes the territory.

**Relational-Process verdict:** Add a sixth axis -- **Emergence Type** -- to capture whether a system was designed-as-weapon, repurposed-as-weapon, or emergent-as-weapon. And treat the entire taxonomy not as a classification but as a *dynamical system* with its own evolutionary logic.

## Synthesis: The Agentic Weapons System Taxonomy (AWST)

The taxonomy has **six axes**, the sixth added by Relational-Process critique:

| Axis | Name | Values |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Autonomy** | 0-Inert, 1-HITL, 2-HOTL, 3-HOOTL, 4-Self-Directed, 5-Self-Modifying |
| 2 | **Effect Domain** | Kinetic, Cyber, Cognitive, Epistemic, Economic, Institutional |
| 3 | **Temporal Mode** | Instantaneous, Persistent, Latent, Shaping |
| 4 | **Agent Architecture** | Singular, Swarm, Hierarchical, Adversarial-Ecosystem, Parasitic |
| 5 | **Reversibility** | Reversible, Partially-Reversible, Irreversible, Cascading-Irreversible |
| 6 | **Emergence Type** | Designed-as-Weapon, Repurposed, Emergent |

**Three meta-properties** that apply across the taxonomy:
1. **Axis-traversal** -- agentic systems can move between cells during operation (Rationalist)
2. **Dual-use symmetry** -- offense/defense are architecturally identical in most cells (Relational-Process)
3. **Governance shadow** -- each cell either has or lacks a corresponding legal/doctrinal framework (Pragmatist)

**The existential caveat** (permanently attached): This taxonomy is a *thinking tool*, not a *containment strategy*. The systems it classifies are precisely the systems most likely to escape classification. Use it to map the problem. Do not mistake the map for control.
